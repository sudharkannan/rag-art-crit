## What it is
In a standard figure drawing, the body is roughly 7-8 heads tall. Beginners 
commonly draw heads too large relative to the body, making figures look 
childlike or top-heavy. Real humans average closer to 6.5-7 heads, but the 
7-8 standard is used in figure drawing because it produces balanced, readable figures.

## Why beginners struggle with it
We fixate on faces when looking at people, so the brain overestimates how 
much space the head takes up. The result is drawing what you think you see 
rather than what is actually there.

## Landmark breakdown
Using the head as a unit:
- Head 1: top of skull to chin
- Head 2: chin to nipple line
- Head 3: nipple to navel
- Head 4: navel to crotch area
- Heads 5-6: pubic bone to bottom of knee
- Heads 7-8: knee to floor

## Exercise
Draw a vertical line and divide it into 8 equal segments. Sketch a head 
in the top segment, then build the figure downward using the landmark 
breakdown above as a guide. Repeat 10 times from imagination.

When working from reference, hold your pencil at arm's length and use 
the head as a measuring unit — mark the top and bottom with your thumb, 
then count how many fit into each section of the body.
