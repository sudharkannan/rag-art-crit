## What it is
Storytelling in a sketch refers to the ability of a drawing to communicate 
something beyond just what a subject looks like — a mood, a moment, a 
relationship, an implied before or after. A drawing with strong storytelling 
makes the viewer ask questions and feel something. A drawing without it 
is just a record of an object or figure.

## Why it matters
Technical accuracy describes what something is. Storytelling makes the 
viewer care about it. Two drawings of the same figure at the same technical 
level will produce completely different responses if one has a clear emotional 
context and the other does not.

## Tools for storytelling in a sketch

- **Pose and gesture**: a slumped shoulder reads as defeat, a raised chin 
  as defiance, a reaching hand as longing. The body communicates emotion 
  before the face does
- **Gaze direction**: where a figure looks implies what they are thinking 
  about. A figure looking off frame creates mystery — what are they looking 
  at? Looking down implies introspection or shame
- **Environment context**: even minimal environmental detail anchors a figure 
  in a world — a single falling leaf, a shadow on the ground, rain lines 
  in the background
- **Implied motion**: a drawing that suggests what just happened or what 
  is about to happen is more engaging than a static neutral pose
- **Negative space**: empty space around a figure can feel lonely, 
  oppressive, or liberating depending on how it is used

## The single moment principle
The strongest storytelling drawings capture a single specific moment rather 
than a generic pose. Not "a person standing" but "a person who just heard 
something surprising and hasn't decided how to react yet." The more specific 
the moment, the more universally it resonates.

## Exercise
Draw the same figure three times in the same basic pose. Change only the 
subtle elements — head angle, shoulder tension, gaze direction, one 
environmental detail. Write one sentence describing the story of each 
version. If you cannot write a different sentence for each, push the 
differences further until you can.
