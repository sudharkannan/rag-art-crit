## What it is
A strong silhouette means your subject is instantly readable as a solid 
black shape — no interior detail needed. If you fill your drawing completely 
black and can still tell what it is and what it is doing, the silhouette 
is working. If it becomes an unreadable blob, the silhouette needs work.

## Why it matters
The silhouette is the first thing the viewer processes before any detail, 
color, or rendering. A weak silhouette means the viewer has to work to 
understand what they are looking at. A strong silhouette communicates 
instantly and makes everything that comes after it more effective.

## What makes a strong silhouette

- **Variety of shapes**: mix large masses with smaller breakup shapes. 
  A figure that is one uniform blob has no visual interest
- **Clear negative space**: the spaces between forms — between an arm and 
  the body, between fingers — are as important as the forms themselves. 
  Closed up negative space collapses the silhouette into a blob
- **Asymmetry**: symmetrical poses produce symmetrical silhouettes which 
  are static and uninteresting. Push one side to contrast the other
- **Pushed extremities**: hands, feet, and head pushed clearly outside 
  the body mass create breakup and interest at the silhouette edge
- **Props and costume**: well designed props and clothing add silhouette 
  complexity without requiring complex anatomy

## The silhouette test
Fill your drawing with a solid flat color — or simply squint until all 
detail disappears. Ask:
- Can I tell what this subject is?
- Can I tell what it is doing?
- Is there variety in the shape — big shapes, medium shapes, small breakups?
- Are there clear pockets of negative space?

If any answer is no, address the silhouette before adding any detail or rendering.

## Exercise
Draw five character poses as pure black silhouettes only — no interior 
lines whatsoever. The silhouette must communicate the pose, mood, and 
subject without any internal detail. Only move to a finished drawing 
once the silhouette reads clearly on its own.
