## What it is
Anatomy landmarks are visible bony points on the body that serve as reliable 
anchors when constructing a figure. You don't need deep anatomy knowledge — 
just a handful of fixed points that keep your figures from feeling rubbery 
and structureless.

## Key landmarks for beginners

- **True shoulder edge**: the actual edge of the shoulder is further out 
  than most beginners place it — it's the bony point where the arm meets 
  the shoulder, not the base of the neck
- **Collarbone line**: the collarbones angle upward from the center of the 
  chest toward the shoulders — drawing them flat makes the chest look stiff
- **Hip bones**: the two bony points on either side of the lower abdomen. 
  Paying attention to their tilt tells you which way the pelvis is rotating — 
  critical for dynamic poses
- **Kneecap**: sits at roughly the halfway point of the leg. Use it as a 
  proportion checkpoint and to clarify which direction the knee is facing
- **Ankle asymmetry**: the inner ankle bone sits higher than the outer — 
  drawing them at the same height makes feet look flat and structureless

## Why it matters
These five points act as fixed anchors on an otherwise soft form. When 
proportions feel off but you can't identify why, checking your landmarks 
is usually the fastest way to find the problem.

## Exercise
Find a photo reference of a standing figure. Before drawing, mark these 
five landmark points directly on the photo. Then construct your figure 
starting from those points rather than from the outline. Notice how much 
more consistent your proportions feel when anchored to fixed points.
