## What it is
Construction is the practice of building a figure or subject from simple 
3D forms — boxes, cylinders, spheres — before adding surface detail. It is 
the alternative to contour drawing, where you draw the outline of a subject 
directly without establishing underlying structure first.

## Construction vs contour
- **Contour drawing**: trace the outline of what you see. Fast but produces 
  flat results because it captures silhouette without volume
- **Construction drawing**: block in the underlying forms first, then wrap 
  the surface around them. Slower but produces figures that feel solid and 
  three dimensional

Most beginner figure drawing problems — flat forms, proportion errors, 
foreshortening failures — are symptoms of drawing contour when construction 
is needed.

## Basic form vocabulary
- **Head**: sphere with a box attached for the jaw
- **Ribcage**: egg shape, slightly flattened
- **Pelvis**: bowl or wedge shape
- **Limbs**: cylinders
- **Hands and feet**: boxes that taper

## Why it improves line confidence
Construction gives every line a purpose. Instead of searching for the 
right outline, you are placing a known form in a known position. Lines 
become deliberate because you know exactly what you are drawing before 
you draw it.

## Exercise
Take a figure drawing you have already done in contour. Redraw it from 
scratch using only basic forms — no outlines, just boxes, cylinders and 
spheres. Don't trace your original. Compare the two versions and note 
where the constructed version feels more solid. Repeat with new drawings 
until construction becomes your default starting point.
