## What it is
Beyond head size, figures have consistent proportional relationships between 
body parts. Common beginner errors include arms that are too short, shoulders 
too narrow, or legs and torso out of balance with each other.

## Key ratios to know
Using the head as a unit (see proportions_head_size):
- **Shoulder width**: roughly 2-3 heads wide for males, 2 heads for females
- **Arm length**: fingertips reach mid-thigh when standing relaxed — the full 
  arm is roughly 3 heads long
- **Hand size**: the hand is roughly the same size as the face from chin to hairline
- **Foot length**: roughly the same as the length of the forearm (elbow to wrist)
- **Torso vs legs**: legs are roughly half the total figure height

## Why beginners struggle with it
Arms are the most commonly shortened body part — the brain underestimates 
how far down the hand actually hangs. Shoulders are frequently drawn too 
narrow, especially on male figures, which makes them look fragile. Legs 
tend to be shortened to fit the page rather than drawn to proportion.

## Exercise
Draw a t-pose figure (arms straight out to the sides) from imagination, 
then check your ratios against the landmarks above. The wingspan 
(fingertip to fingertip) should roughly equal the total height of the figure. 
Correct and redraw. Repeat until it feels natural.
